Now that AI assisted coding is a thing, any semi-capable coder is a software engineer.  Proactive folk are getting on and just building what previously they would need to convince management to fund a developer team to work on as a POC. The upside of this is a proliferation of ideas suddenly materialising.  

![AI Side Projects](/static/images/rise-of-ai-side-projects.png)

Its interesting then, to observe what is being built, as this indicates market gaps, product shortcomings or simply a lack of awareness of existing product functionality.  

Sure, the many POCs being showcased online recently are probably not production grade or ready to distribute as a product, but Product Managers everywhere should be salivating at this open, in depth and honest assessment of existing product ranges, and the opportunities that provides.  

The low hanging fruit here are simple public web apps that either:  
 - Have no backend, or perhaps just read some data from blob storage or something equally simple.  
 - Are built on top of an existing mature and robust platform backend that provides those tricky parts like authentication, identity, databases and content management.  

 For the first point, simple mapping applications that act like dashboards are a great example, and I especially love all the examples of map apps reading direct from geoparquet. There is no authentication required or backend API to wrangle with, so the producer of the app can focus on UI and visualisation to create a great end user experience.  

 For the second point, apps built on top of ArcGIS Online or ArcGIS Enterprise are good examples. Authentication and identity are tricky at the best of times and the consequences of getting those wrong can be severe. Web services for geospatial data are not straight forward to implement or scale. 

Because of my role, I'm seeing a lot of system administration tools, ETL orchestrators and custom dashboard or visualization tools. Take a look around your network and make a note of what is being built, look past any issues with quality or robustness and focus for a moment on the ideas and concepts. Consider the opportunities being identified at this time as heaps of AI powered side projects appear.
